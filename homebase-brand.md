## TYPOGRAPHY

We use two fonts. **Plus Jakarta Sans is the primary, everyday font** — use it by default for almost everything. **Druk is the display font** — reserved for high-impact headline moments. When in doubt, use Plus Jakarta Sans.

### Workhorse: Plus Jakarta Sans ← USE THIS BY DEFAULT
- Google Font: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Plus+Jakarta+Sans
- **Headlines:** Extra Bold · 112pt · 92% line height · -2 tracking
- **Campaign headlines:** 48pt · -2 tracking
- **Body copy:** Medium · 16pt · 170% line height
- Use Medium (not Regular) — naturally lighter structure needs the weight for readability
- ⚠️ **Never set Plus Jakarta Sans in all caps** unless explicitly asked. It is a mixed-case font.

### Display: Druk ← USE ONLY WHEN SPECIFICALLY REQUESTED
- Font files (licensed): https://www.dropbox.com/scl/fo/ee55p7t3vnmm2rrp7w7hk/h?rlkey=6tkemehc88seq3kx0cy3rhsbn&st=2a661lhj&dl=0
- Size: 164pt · Line height: 86%
- **Druk is ALWAYS uppercase.** No exceptions.
- Use: H1s, campaign headlines, presentation titles, any moment needing maximum impact
- **Functional** (readability): Druk Bold only
- **Expressive** (artistic/creative): Any Druk weight — Condensed, Wide — for visual expression
- ⚠️ Druk Condensed and Wide are expressive only, never for functional/readable text

## MOTION & ANIMATION

Motion should feel like it belongs in the real world — not a cartoon, not a demo reel. Every animation should reinforce trust and ease, not call attention to itself.

### Guiding Principle
**Natural, not noticed.** The best animation is one users don't consciously register — it just feels right. Motion confirms actions, guides attention, and adds life without ever feeling exaggerated, whimsical, or performative.

### UI & Product Animations
These should feel like real software interactions:
- **Smooth and physical** — elements ease in and out as if they have natural weight. Use ease-in-out curves (avoid linear or aggressive bounce).
- **Proportional timing** — small elements move quickly (150–200ms), larger transitions take slightly longer (250–350ms). Nothing lingers.
- **No exaggeration** — no overshooting, no rubber-banding, no dramatic entrances. A dropdown opens; it doesn't spring into existence.
- **Expected behavior** — motion should confirm what the user already anticipated. It should never surprise.
- ✅ Smooth easing · Subtle fades · Natural transitions
- ❌ Bouncy physics · Cartoony overshoots · Attention-grabbing entrances

### Hand-Drawn Element Animations
When animating the brand's hand-drawn marks, stickers, or illustrated elements:
- **Draw-on feel** — elements should appear as if someone is naturally writing or sketching them in real time. The motion follows the natural stroke path.
- **No hands, no theatrics** — the drawing effect is implied through the stroke reveal, not illustrated with a hand icon or exaggerated gesture.
- **Organic pacing** — speed should mirror a real pen moving across paper: slightly irregular, not mechanically uniform.
- **One stroke at a time** — reveal elements sequentially and simply. Don't animate too many drawn elements at once.
- ✅ Stroke reveal · Natural pen rhythm · Handwritten feel
- ❌ Robotic uniformity · Multiple simultaneous draws · Theatrical flourishes

### Overall Rules
- All motion — UI or illustrative — should feel **normal, natural, and expected**.
- Never use animation to decorate. Use it to communicate.
- When in doubt, slow it down slightly and reduce the amount of movement — restraint is always on-brand.

## VOICE & TONE

### The Three Voice Qualities (Always On)

#### 1. Neighborly — Helpful · Relatable · Accessible
Be a neighbor they can lean on. Show deep respect for small businesses and the people behind them.
- Write like you talk. No jargon. Conversational, not slangy.
- Don't force humor — use wit when it lands naturally.
- Be inclusive — every industry, background, ability.
- ✅ Empathetic · Conversational · Relatable
- ❌ Judgemental · Clinical · Detached

#### 2. Clear — Straightforward · Direct · Transparent
Save people time. Prioritize what's most valuable.
- Write for someone seeing Homebase for the first time.
- Lead with what matters most. Cut anything not useful.
- Keep it short. Respect attention.
- ✅ Informative · Direct · Concise
- ❌ Overwhelming · Meandering · Sparse

#### 3. Proactive — Confident · Ready · Reliable
Reflect steady passion for problem-solving. Move teams forward.
- Show, don't tell. Use real behavior, stats, customers.
- Bring energy with purpose — optimistic but real. Exclamation points sparingly.
- Find fresh angles. Avoid repeating the same phrases.
- ✅ Confident · Enthusiastic · Innovative
- ❌ Know-it-all · Over-the-top · Eccentric

## BACKGROUNDS

Backgrounds should support content, never overpower it. Approved background treatments:
- Solid: White Stripes, Grey 01, Deep Purple, Purple Haze
- Subtle gradient applications (Purple family gradients)
- Photography with warm overlay when placing text
- Spectrum gradient: AI contexts only, never full-page

### Texture & Noise
A subtle layer of noise or grain can be added to solid color, photography, or gradient backgrounds to make them feel more tangible and grounded. The texture should not be noticeable at a glance — it's felt more than seen, adding just enough surface quality to prevent backgrounds from feeling flat or digital.

- **Subtle noise overlay** — a low-opacity grain layer on top of solid or gradient backgrounds. Works especially well on deep purple and dark backgrounds.
- **Keep it restrained** — if you can clearly see the grain, it's too much. It should read as texture, not noise.
- Never apply heavy grain that competes with content or makes text harder to read.

### Textured Grainy Gradients
In some contexts — hero sections, editorial moments, bold campaign visuals — we use a richer background treatment: organic blurred shapes with a grainy gradient applied on top.

- **How it works:** Start with large, soft blurred shapes (think diffused color blobs or radial glows) in brand colors, overlaid or blended together. Then apply a grain/noise texture over the entire composition.
- **The result:** A gradient that feels organic, handcrafted, and tactile rather than clean and digital.
- Colors should stay within the brand palette — typically Deep Purple, Purple Haze, and Purple Rain, with occasional Pink Floyd or Yellowcard accents.
- The grain should unify the composition — it sits on top of everything and makes the whole background read as one cohesive surface.
- ✅ Organic · Tactile · Atmospheric · Feels crafted
- ❌ Overly sharp · Heavy noise · Distracting from content

### Animated Grainy Gradient Backgrounds
When a grainy gradient background needs to move — for video, motion ads, or animated digital contexts — use **Paper Design's Grain Gradient shader tool** to generate the animation.

**Tool:** https://shaders.paper.design/grain-gradient

This tool produces smooth, organic animated gradients with grain baked in. Configure it using Homebase brand colors and keep motion settings subtle and slow — consistent with our overall motion principles (natural, not noticed).

**Recommended starting point for brand-aligned output:**
- `colors`: Purple Haze + Purple Rain (`7E3DD4`, `D5C7FF`)
- `colorBack`: Deep Purple (`1E0B3A`)
- `softness`: 0.5
- `intensity`: 0.5
- `noise`: 0.25
- `speed`: 1 (increase only slightly for more energy — never go fast)
- `shape`: corners or organic

Adjust colors and softness per context, but always stay within the brand palette. The animation should drift and breathe — never pulse, flash, or call attention to itself.
