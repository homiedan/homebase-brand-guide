Welcome

These guidelines are your source of truth for how our brand looks, sounds, and shows up in the world. Here, you'll find our brand elements, visual examples, and guidance for creating work.

Homebase

Homebase strives to make hourly work easier for every small business. Our app brings together scheduling, time tracking, and payroll—using AI thoughtfully to help teams focus less on admin and more on the work that matters.

Brand

Our Homebase brand is about what we do, make, and say. How we present ourselves to the world. The more cohesive and consistent our efforts are, the more we resonate with—and convert—our audience.

Our brand promise

To make hourly work easier for every small business.

Logo

Our logo, logotype and logomark, should never be altered or modified in any way. It may only appear in Purple Haze or white—no other colors are permitted. The logotype should never be outlined, stylized, or adjusted, and must always appear as a single, unified color without being divided or separated.

Logotype

The wordmark — homebase in lowercase — is our primary logo. Use Purple Haze on light backgrounds, White on dark backgrounds. No other colors, ever.

Logomark

The h icon is reserved for small spaces and app icons only. Never use it alongside the logotype — it's a standalone symbol for contexts where the full wordmark won't fit.

Do
  • Always use the full lowercase logotype as the primary mark
  • Use Purple Haze on light or white backgrounds
  • Use White on dark or purple backgrounds
  • Maintain generous clear space around the logo on all sides
  • Use the h icon only in small-space or app icon contexts
Don't
  • Capitalize or alter the casing of the logotype in any way
  • Apply outlines, strokes, drop shadows, or glows
  • Rotate, skew, or distort the logo
  • Place on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
  • Use any color other than Purple Haze or White
  • Pair the h icon alongside the full logotype
Cobranding

We collaborate with many partners, and our campaigns often feature both our logo and a partner's logo. In these cases, the two logos should be separated by an "X", with equal spacing between each logo and the "X" to maintain balance and clarity.

Separation

Always use an "X" between the homebase logotype and the partner logo. Maintain equal spacing on both sides of the "X" — the spacing from each logo to the "X" should be identical.

Sizing

Pay close attention to sizing—logos should be adjusted so they appear optically equal, ensuring neither brand outweighs the other.

AI Branding

Assets promoting our AI Assistants use our spectrum gradient to add energy and relevancy. The sparkles icon further distinguishes AI Assistants from our other offerings.

✦ Sparkle Icon

The four-pointed sparkle (✦) marks AI-powered features and surfaces. It signals "this is AI" without over-explaining. Use it as a small badge or inline indicator next to AI feature labels and entry points.

Spectrum Gradient

Deep Purple → Purple Haze → Pink Floyd → Yellowcard. This gradient is exclusively for AI branding contexts — do not use it anywhere else. It expresses intelligence, energy, and possibility.

Typography

Two typefaces carry everything. Druk sets the tone — big, bold, unmistakable. Plus Jakarta Sans does the everyday work, readable and human at every size.

Plus Jakarta Sans Medium · 16pt / 170% line height · Default for all body copy, captions, UI text, and anything below headline level

Druk — Impact & Display

Bold is the only weight for functional use (H1s, headlines, titles). Condensed and Wide are expressive cuts — artistic moments only. Druk should feel like it commands attention without shouting.

Plus Jakarta Sans — Everyday Workhorse

Extra Bold for all headline levels below Druk. Medium for body, captions, and UI copy. This typeface carries the vast majority of our communication across product, marketing, and social.

Graphic Elements

A handful of graphic tools add personality and energy without overwhelming the message. Use them with intention — less is always more.

Large Icons

Large icons are used as small visual accents to emphasize key messages or to add subtle background interest. Apply them thoughtfully to avoid overwhelming the layout.

Hand-Drawn Elements

Hand-drawn marks help draw attention to specific content. Use them sparingly to maintain clarity and avoid visual clutter.

Stickers

Stickers add depth, personality, and moments of emphasis. They should be used selectively and never in excess.

Emojis

Emojis introduce energy and informality to certain pieces. Use them with restraint to ensure they enhance the message rather than distract from it.

Backgrounds

We use a range of background styles—from textured and photo backgrounds to solid or purple fields. Regardless of the style, backgrounds should always support the content placed on top of them. They should never overpower the artwork or typography, but instead complement and balance it, ensuring the message remains clear and visually accessible.

Product

Product visuals are used in two ways: full, detailed screens to demonstrate functionality, and simplified representations to add context without overwhelming the layout.

Full, Detailed Screens

We use full-scale, detailed product screens to clearly demonstrate what our product looks like and how it works. These are shown at a larger size to highlight functionality and design. Full product views are also used in context—on laptops, tablets, or handheld devices within photography—to show the product in real-life usage.

Simplified Screens

We also use smaller or less detailed representations of the product to add subtle background messages or context. These convey core functionality without requiring full interface detail and work well in layouts where space or clarity is a priority.

Photography

Our photography celebrates the people who make small businesses run: owners, employees, and teams. We want people to look natural—everyday heroes who are relatable and inspiring. They should be candid, warm, and friendly.

People

Feature small business owners, employees, and teams (groups).

Products

Highlight what the business sells or makes. Keep it simple.

Spaces

Capture the space and special details to give a sense of place and personality.

Do
  • Use natural light whenever possible — avoid harsh midday sun
  • Keep retouching light and realistic; preserve natural skin tones
  • Apply warm color overlay (R210 G205 B200) when placing type over photos
  • Show real environments, real people, real moments
  • Choose images with warm, inviting color temperatures
Don't
  • Use overly staged or corporate-looking photography
  • Apply heavy filters, extreme color grading, or artificial looks
  • Choose stock images with forced smiles or artificial scenarios
  • Place type directly on photos without the warm color overlay
  • Feature environments or people that don't reflect our customer base
Voice & Tone

Voice is how Homebase always sounds and how we keep the brand consistent. No matter where we appear, we are:

Neighborly
Helpful · Relatable · Accessible

We have deep respect for small businesses and the people behind them. Show up as a neighbor they can always lean on. Be kind, approachable, and use everyday language familiar to a wide range of teams.

Clear
Straightforward · Direct · Transparent

We take pride in saving people time and have compassion for the people before them. When a message is easily understood, it's easier to act on. Prioritize what's most valuable to the reader.

Proactive
Confident · Ready · Relatable

We're dedicated to making work easier for small businesses. Our voice should reflect our steady passion for problem-solving: confident, reassuring, and focused on helping teams move forward.

Our voice is constant. Our tone is how we can adjust our voice for specific moments across the customer journey:
